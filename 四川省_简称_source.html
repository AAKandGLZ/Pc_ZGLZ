China is undeniably leading the way in driving data center developments in the region. With a robust market and increasing demand for data center services, China has seen significant growth and investment in this sector. 

As one of the largest state-owned telecommunications companies in China, China Telecom Corporation is a key player in the country's data center sector. They have been instrumental in expanding and enhancing data center infrastructure to meet the growing needs of businesses and consumers. 

China has implemented strategic plans to boost computing power and establish data center clusters in key areas. The construction of eight computing hubs and ten data center clusters in eastern and western China is part of the government's cross-regional plan to strengthen the country's computing capabilities. 

These efforts are further bolstered by the release of industry-related policies, such as the Three-year Action Plan for New Data, which aims to support and regulate the data center industry. With these initiatives, China is solidifying its position as a leader in data center development within the region. 

Industry reports states that the revenue generated in the China colocation market is expected to reach a significant milestone by the end of 2023. China's data center market is projected to generate a revenue of approximately US$69 billion by the end of the year. This growth is attributed to the increasing demand for data center services and the country's focus on technological advancements. 

The China Internet Data Center Market is also expected to reach a value of USD 64.01 billion in 2023, further highlighting the substantial revenue potential of the colocation market in China.  

Top Data Center Locations in China

China is home to several top cities that play a crucial role in the Chinese colocation market. Shanghai, Beijing, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, and Chengdu are among the largest and most important cities in China when it comes to data center infrastructure and colocation services. 

Shanghai, with its thriving economy and status as a global financial hub, attracts a significant amount of data center investment. It has a robust telecommunications network, reliable power supply, and a favorable business environment, making it an ideal location for businesses seeking colocation services in China. 

Beijing, as the capital city of China, is a major hub for technology and innovation. It hosts numerous data centers and provides access to a vast talent pool of IT professionals. Beijing's strategic location and advanced infrastructure make it an attractive destination for both domestic and international businesses looking to establish their presence in the Chinese colocation market. 

Guangzhou, located in southern China, is another key city in the colocation market. It serves as a gateway to the Pearl River Delta, a region known for its strong manufacturing and technology industries. With its proximity to Hong Kong and Macau, Guangzhou offers excellent connectivity and is an important hub for businesses operating in southern China. 

Shenzhen, also located in the Pearl River Delta, is renowned for its vibrant tech industry and entrepreneurial spirit. It is home to many leading technology companies and has a robust digital infrastructure, making it an ideal location for colocation services. Shenzhen's strategic location near Hong Kong and its supportive business environment make it a popular choice for businesses seeking colocation solutions in southern China. 

Chengdu, located in southwestern China, is emerging as a key player in the colocation market. The city's government has been actively promoting its digital economy, attracting investments and fostering a favorable business environment. With its lower costs of operation and abundant energy resources, Chengdu offers an enticing option for businesses looking to establish data centers and leverage colocation services in western China. 

These top cities in China have established themselves as vital hubs for the Chinese colocation market, providing businesses with the necessary infrastructure, connectivity, and support to thrive in the digital age.
